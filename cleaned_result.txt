What to Know About Thumb Arthritis Thumb arthritis occurs when cartilage in the thumb joint wears down, causing pain, stiffness, and reduced motion, often with age. What Are the Types of Spondyloarthropathies? Spondyloarthropathies are spine-related inflammatory arthritis types like ankylosing spondylitis and psoriatic arthritis. Non-Radiographic Axial Spondyloarthritis Non-radiographic axial spondyloarthritis causes spine inflammation and pain without joint damage seen on X-rays. Conditions That Can Seem Like Ankylosing Spondylitis Several common conditions can mimic ankylosing spondylitis symptoms, making diagnosis tricky and sometimes delayed. What Is Facet Arthrosis? Facet arthrosis causes lower back pain from worn joints. Learn the symptoms, how it's diagnosed, and treatment options. Avascular Necrosis (AVN or Osteonecrosis) Avascular necrosis causes bone tissue death from poor blood flow, often leading to pain, joint damage, or bone collapse. Shoulder Impingement Syndrome Shoulder impingement causes pain from tendon or bursa compression, often worsened by overhead activity or repetitive motion. Polymyalgia Rheumatica and Temporal Arteritis Polymyalgia rheumatica causes morning stiffness and pain in shoulders and hips and may lead to serious artery inflammation. Polymyositis: Causes, Symptoms, and Treatment Polymyositis causes muscle weakness and pain, making daily tasks hard. Treatment can ease symptoms and restore strength. Tenosynovitis Tenosynovitis is tendon sheath inflammation causing joint pain, swelling, and stiffness—often treatable with rest and medications. What Is Olecranon Bursitis? Elbow bursitis causes swelling and pain at the tip of your elbow, often from pressure or injury. Treatment helps ease symptoms. What Is Scapholunate Advanced Collapse (SLAC Wrist)? SLAC wrist is a painful joint condition caused by ligament damage leading to arthritis and limited wrist movement. What Is Kienbock’s Disease? Kienbock’s disease causes wrist pain and stiffness due to poor blood flow to a key bone leading to its gradual collapse. Familial Mediterranean Fever (FMF) FMF is a rare inherited disorder that causes recurring fever attacks and joint pain mostly in people of Mediterranean descent. Baker’s Cyst: Causes, Symptoms, & Treatment A Baker’s cyst is a fluid-filled lump behind the knee that may cause swelling, pain, and stiffness. Scleroderma Symptoms Scleroderma causes tight, thickened skin and may affect organs like the lungs, heart, or digestive system. Gout What Are Gout and Pseudogout?
